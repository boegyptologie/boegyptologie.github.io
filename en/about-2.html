Bibliothèque Ouverte d’Égyptologie (BOE) is a scientific, open-access, independent journal, with long-term preservation of its publications, dedicated to the study of ancient Egypt in all its forms. It welcomes articles of up to 60,000 characters (including spaces and footnotes) on any topic related to Egyptology, from prehistory to the Coptic period. The journal values diversity and originality of approaches, as well as scientific multilingualism (French, English, German, Spanish, Portuguese, and others upon request).

Open access, long-term preservation, and independence

Publishing in BOE is free of charge for authors, who also retain their rights under the CC BY 4.0 license. Articles are freely accessible on Zenodo, an open-access institutional repository managed by CERN and supported by the European Union. This platform ensures long-term preservation and stable identification of publications via DOI, regardless of the future of BOE’s editorial structure.

The journal’s editorial policy is detailed and publicly available (cf. our transparency and policies statement), in accordance with the requirements of the Directory of Open Access Journals (DOAJ), a recognized international index that ensures scientific integrity, independence, and transparency. This editorial rigor helps limit biases related to endogeny: no more than 25% of published articles may be authored by members of the editorial or scientific board.

Who are we?

The journal is published by Bibliothèque Ouverte des Mondes Anciens (BOMA), an independent, non-profit publishing initiative committed to the free dissemination of knowledge. Founded by three Egyptology graduates, it is grounded in a collaborative dynamic that ensures the journal’s scientific and editorial direction.
