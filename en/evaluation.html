Editorial Review·

a. The article is properly anonymized.
⚠ Inform the Editor-in-Chief of any acknowledgments and self-citations in the comments section below. The PDF's metadata may contain the author's name. In such cases, re-export the file from the original editable version (e.g., Word, LibreOffice, or similar), making sure that no author information is included in the file properties.
b. The text does not exceed 60,000 characters (including footnotes and spaces; the end bibliography, if any, is not counted).
c. Typography and bibliographic referencing are consistent (any style/convention is acceptable, as long as it is applied consistently).
d. The clarity of the text and the logical flow of ideas are satisfactory (as distinct from stylistic or aesthetic concerns: indicate only what is essential for comprehension on a first reading).
e. The argumentation is properly supported by cited references.
f. The article is free of broad generalities such as "at all times."
g. The quality of the abstract is satisfactory (between 450 and 1,500 characters, including spaces).
h. The article is not plagiarized, using a simple Google search with selected excerpts placed in quotation marks.
